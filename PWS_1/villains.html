Villains: The Complete Cast

As time went along, Dumile branched his musical releases, creating different personas other than the supervillain that served as channels for different musical approaches. Also, the collaborations with other musicians are sometimes a character of their own.

The personality split began in 2003, when Dumile released the album Take Me To Your Leader under the pseudonym King Geedorah, an album that continued the trend of incuding TV and movie samples. Next came Viktor Vaughn with his acclaimed album Vaudeville Villain, a character that represents a London crook affiliated with MF Doom.

After a short hiatus, MF Doom came back with his second album, MM..Food, an anagram of MF DOOM that represents a concept album revolving around the different favorite foods of the supervillain (in case you're wondering, it's Gumbo* and Guiness beer among others), loaded with punchlines and satirical commentary on the state of rap at the time. It was chosen by Complex* magazine as one of the 25 best concept hip-hop albums of all time due to its "uniqueness and unorthodox approach while still maintaining a vibrant quality and cohesive structure".
